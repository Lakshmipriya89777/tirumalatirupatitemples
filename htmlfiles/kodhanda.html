About The Temple : Sri Kodandarama Swami Temple is one of the oldest and most revered temples in Tirupati, Andhra Pradesh. Situated near Kapila Theertham and close to the Tirupati town center, the temple is dedicated to Lord Rama, worshipped here as Kodandarama—the divine archer holding the Kodanda (bow).
Mythological Significance
According to tradition, Lord Rama, along with Sita and Lakshmana, rested at this sacred spot during His journey after the return from Lanka. The temple’s presiding deities—Rama, Sita, and Lakshmana—are depicted together, symbolizing dharma, devotion, and righteous living. The presence of Hanuman in a devoted posture further enhances the temple’s spiritual importance and strong connection to the Ramayana.
Historical Background
The temple is believed to have been constructed during the Vijayanagara period (around the 10th–14th centuries), with inscriptions and architectural elements pointing to the patronage of Vijayanagara rulers. Over the centuries, the temple served as an important center of Vaishnavite worship in Tirupati, even before Tirupati grew into a major pilgrimage town. The temple is currently administered by the Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams (TTD), ensuring the continuation of traditional rituals and maintenance.
Temple Architecture
The Kodandarama Swami Temple showcases classic Dravidian-style architecture, featuring:
*A beautifully carved gopuram
*Spacious mandapams
*Intricately sculpted pillars and stonework
The sanctum houses idols of Sri Rama, Sita, Lakshmana, and Hanuman, all carved with great artistic detail.
Spiritual Importance
Devotees visit the temple to seek:
*Blessings for righteous living
*Family harmony and peace
*Strength, courage, and devotion
*Relief from obstacles
Special importance is given to Sri Rama Navami, Hanuman Jayanti, and Vaikunta Ekadashi, when the temple is beautifully decorated and attracts large numbers of devotees.
Present-Day Importance
Today, the Sri Kodandarama Swami Temple remains a vital spiritual landmark in Tirupati. Its historical depth, serene atmosphere, and deep connection to the Ramayana make it a cherished destination for devotees and tourists alike.
