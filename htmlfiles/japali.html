About The Temple : Japali Theertham, also known as Japali Hanuman Temple, is a sacred and serene place located deep inside the forests of Tirumala Hills, near Tirupati, Andhra Pradesh. This holy site is dedicated to Lord Hanuman and is closely associated with Saint Japali Maharishi, a great sage mentioned in ancient Hindu scriptures. Japali Theertham is surrounded by dense greenery and offers a peaceful spiritual atmosphere, making it a special place for devotees seeking silence, meditation, and devotion.
History of Japali Theertham
According to legend, Japali Maharishi performed intense penance (tapasya) at this place. Pleased with his devotion, Lord Hanuman appeared before him and blessed him. Because of this divine event, the place came to be known as Japali Theertham. It is believed that Lord Rama, during his exile (Vanavasa), visited this sacred spot along with Lakshmana and Hanuman. The theertham (holy water stream) flowing here is considered very pure and sacred. The Hanuman idol worshipped here is believed to be self-manifested (Swayambhu), adding to the spiritual importance of the temple.
Religious Significance
Japali Theertham holds a special place in the Tirumala pilgrimage. Devotees believe that:
*Bathing or sprinkling water from Japali Theertham removes sins
*Praying to Lord Hanuman here grants strength, courage, and protection
*The place is ideal for meditation and spiritual practice
Many pilgrims visit Japali Theertham before or after Lord Venkateswara darshan.
Temple Features & Natural Beauty
The Japali Hanuman Temple is simple in structure, blending naturally with the forest surroundings. A small stream flows near the temple, creating a calm and divine environment. The sound of water and forest silence adds to the spiritual experience. The temple area is well-maintained by Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams (TTD).
Festivals & Worship
Special poojas are performed on:
*Hanuman Jayanti
*Rama Navami
*Vaikunta Ekadasi
On these days, many devotees visit Japali Theertham to seek blessings.
Location & Access
Japali Theertham is located about 5–6 km from Tirumala main temple. Devotees can reach the place by vehicle up to a point and then walk a short distance through forest paths.
Timings:
Morning: 6:00 AM – 1:00 PM
Afternoon / Evening: 2:00 PM – 7:00 PM
