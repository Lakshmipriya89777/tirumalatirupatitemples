About The Temple : Sri Prasanna Venkateswara Swamy Temple also known as Appalayagunta temple is an ancient and famous Hindu temple located near Tirupati in Tirupati district, Andhra Pradesh. The temple is around 16 km from Tirupati and 7 km from Renigunta, and it comes under the administration of Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams (TTD). The presiding deity of the temple is Lord Venkateswara, who is worshipped here in a pleasant and calm (prasanna) form. Unlike Tirumala, where the Lord appears majestic and powerful, the deity at Appalayagunta is believed to bless devotees with kindness, peace, and satisfaction.
History of the Temple
According to temple legends, Lord Venkateswara stopped at Appalayagunta while returning to Tirumala after his marriage with Goddess Padmavati at Narayanavanam. It is believed that the Lord rested at this place and appeared in a calm and happy mood, which is why the deity here is known as Prasanna Venkateswara Swamy. The temple has a long history and was developed during the Vijayanagara period, with later contributions from various rulers and devotees. Over time, the temple gained importance as a sacred pilgrimage center for devotees visiting Tirupati.
Religious Importance
Many devotees believe that darshan at Appalayagunta completes the Tirupati pilgrimage. It is also said that devotees who are unable to visit Tirumala can worship Lord Venkateswara here and receive similar spiritual blessings. The temple is especially popular for:
*Marriage-related prayers
*Peace and prosperity
*Removal of obstacles in life
