About The Temple : The ISKCON Temple (International Society for Krishna Consciousness) is a well-known spiritual center dedicated to Lord Krishna. ISKCON temples are found across the world, including in India, and they play an important role in spreading Bhakti Yoga, devotion, and Vedic culture. The ISKCON temple near Tirupati is popularly known as Sri Sri Radha Govinda Mandir. It is located near Alipiri / Tirupati foothills, making it easily accessible to pilgrims visiting Tirumala and Tirupati.
History of ISKCON
ISKCON was founded in 1966 in New York City by His Divine Grace A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada, a great spiritual teacher and follower of Sri Chaitanya Mahaprabhu. His mission was to spread the teachings of the Bhagavad Gita and Srimad Bhagavatam across the world. Under his guidance, ISKCON grew into a global movement with hundreds of temples, cultural centers, and educational institutions in more than 100 countries.
Religious & Spiritual Importance
The ISKCON temple worships Lord Krishna along with Radha Rani, emphasizing pure devotion (bhakti). Daily worship includes:
*Mangala Aarti
*Japa (chanting Hare Krishna Mahamantra)
Bhagavad Gita and Bhagavatam classes
Kirtans and Bhajans
Devotees believe that visiting the ISKCON temple helps in developing peace of mind, spiritual discipline, and devotion.
Temple Architecture & Activities
ISKCON temples follow traditional Vaishnava architectural style blended with modern design. The temple atmosphere is calm and devotional, filled with Vedic chants and kirtans. The ISKCON Tirupati temple also conducts:
*Free prasadam distribution
*Spiritual lectures and youth programs
*Festivals like Janmashtami, Radhashtami, and Gaura Purnima
Role in Tirupati Pilgrimage
Many pilgrims visiting Tirupati include ISKCON temple in their journey, as it provides a peaceful spiritual experience focused on Lord Krishna’s teachings, complementing the Vaishnava tradition of Tirumala.
