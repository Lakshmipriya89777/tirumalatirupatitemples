About The Temple : Sri Parasurameswara Swamy Temple also known as Gudimallam temple is one of the most ancient and historically significant temples in India. It is located in Gudimallam village, about 13 km from Tirupati in Tirupati district, Andhra Pradesh. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva, worshipped here as Parasurameswara Swamy. What makes Gudimallam temple unique is the presence of one of the oldest Shiva Lingas in the world, dating back to around the 2nd century BCE. This temple is not only a place of worship but also an important archaeological and cultural heritage site.
History of the Temple
The Gudimallam Shiva Linga is believed to be over 2,000 years old and is considered one of the earliest known representations of Lord Shiva in human form. The linga has a beautifully carved figure of Lord Shiva holding a water pot (kamandalu) and a deer, standing on a dwarf figure known as Apasmara Purusha, symbolizing ignorance. Historians and archaeologists believe that the temple developed over different periods, including the Satavahana, Pallava, Chola, and Vijayanagara dynasties. Inscriptions found in and around the temple indicate royal patronage and continuous worship through centuries. The temple’s name, Gudimallam, comes from the Telugu words “Gudi” (temple) and “Malla” (wrestler or strong person), reflecting ancient local traditions.
Religious & Cultural Importance
Gudimallam temple represents a rare blend of Shaiva and ancient fertility worship traditions. Unlike typical Shiva lingas found in later temples, the Gudimallam linga is distinctly anthropomorphic, showing Lord Shiva in a realistic human form. Devotees believe that worshipping Lord Parasurameswara Swamy here brings:
*Removal of sins and negative energies
*Strength and spiritual growth
*Relief from ancestral and life obstacles
The temple attracts devotees, historians, archaeologists, and tourists from across India and abroad.
Architecture & Temple Features
The temple architecture is simple yet powerful, reflecting early stone temple traditions. The sanctum is built to allow the Shiva Linga to receive water from a natural spring, which continuously flows over it. The temple also has shrines for Goddess Parvati and Lord Subramanya. The surroundings of the temple are peaceful, with natural greenery adding to its spiritual atmosphere.
Festivals & Worship
Major festivals celebrated at Gudimallam Temple include:
*Maha Shivaratri
*Pradosham
*Karthika Masam
Special abhishekams and poojas are performed during these occasions.
