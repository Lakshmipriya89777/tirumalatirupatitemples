About The Temple : Kapila Theertham is a famous and sacred temple located at the foothills of the Tirumala Hills in Tirupati. It is the only temple in Tirupati dedicated to Lord Shiva and is also known as Sri Kapileswara Swamy Temple.
History:
Kapila Theertham is believed to be very ancient and is associated with the great sage Kapila Maharshi. According to legend:
*Sage Kapila performed deep penance (tapas) at this place.
*Lord Shiva appeared before him in the form of a Swayambhu Lingam (self-manifested Shiva Lingam).
*The sacred water body (Theertham) near the temple is believed to have divine origin.
The temple gained importance during the rule of the Vijayanagara Empire, and many additions were made to the structure during that period.
Waterfall & Theertham:
One of the most beautiful features of Kapila Theertham is the waterfall, especially during the monsoon season.
*The waterfall flows from the Tirumala hills.
*The water collects in a holy pond near the temple.
*Devotees take a holy dip here before entering the temple.
During Maha Shivaratri, special poojas and celebrations are conducted, and thousands of devotees visit.
Temple Architecture:
*Built in traditional Dravidian style.
*The temple has a beautiful Gopuram (tower) at the entrance.
*The main deity is Lord Kapileswara Swamy (Shiva).
*There are also shrines for Goddess Kamakshi, Lord Ganesha, and other deities.
Spiritual Importance:
*It is considered very powerful for removing sins and obstacles.
*Devotees believe that offering prayers here brings peace and prosperity.
*It is an important pilgrimage spot for both Shiva and Tirumala devotees.
Timings:
*Suprabhatam (Morning wake-up prayer): 5:00 AM – 5:30 AM
*Abhishekam (Holy bathing of deity): 5:30 AM – 6:30 AM
*Alankaram & Archana (Decoration & prayer): 6:30 AM – 7:00 AM
*Sarva Darshanam (General darshan):
• Morning: ~7:00 AM – 4:00 PM
• Evening: ~5:30 PM – 8:00 PM
*Ekantha Seva (Night prayer): ~8:00 PM – 8:15 PM
*These timings can vary slightly during festivals like Maha Shivaratri or Kartika Purnima and on special occasions.
*It’s best to arrive early in the morning if you want to attend the rituals from the beginning.
