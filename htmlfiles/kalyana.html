About The Temple : Sri Kalyana Venkateswara Swamy Temple (Srinivasa Mangapuram) is a small town located about 12 km from Tirupati in Andhra Pradesh. The temple is situated at the foothills of the Tirumala hills and is easily accessible by road.
History
The temple is dedicated to Lord Kalyana Venkateswara Swamy, a form of Lord Vishnu. According to legend, after marrying Goddess Padmavathi at Narayanavanam, Lord Venkateswara stayed at Srinivasa Mangapuram for some time before proceeding to Tirumala. Because of this divine stay after marriage, the deity here is known as Kalyana (married) Venkateswara Swamy. The temple is believed to have been constructed during the rule of the Vijayanagara dynasty, particularly under the patronage of King Tallapaka Annamacharya’s descendants and later rulers. The idol in this temple is considered one of the tallest and most majestic forms of Lord Venkateswara, standing about 8 feet tall.
Temple Significance
Devotees believe that worshipping here brings marital harmony and helps in resolving marriage-related delays.
Newly married couples often visit the temple to seek blessings.
It is considered highly auspicious to visit this temple before going to Tirumala.
The temple is maintained by Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams (TTD).
Important Festivals
Annual Brahmotsavam
Vaikunta Ekadasi
Kalyanotsavam (Celestial wedding ceremony)
Architectural Style
The temple follows traditional South Indian temple architecture with:
A beautiful Rajagopuram (entrance tower)
Spacious prakaram (temple courtyard)
Sculpted pillars and mandapam
