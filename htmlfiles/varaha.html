About The Temple : The Varaha Swami Temple is a very important temple in Tirumala, Andhra Pradesh. It is dedicated to Lord Varaha, who is one form (avatar) of Lord Vishnu. Lord Varaha has the face of a boar and the body of a human. This temple is located near the holy water tank called Swami Pushkarini, very close to the famous Lord Venkateswara (Balaji) Temple. Long ago, it is believed that Lord Varaha saved Mother Earth (Bhudevi) from a demon who took her deep into the ocean. After saving her, Lord Varaha chose Tirumala hills as his place to stay. Because of this, Tirumala was first known as “Adi Varaha Kshetra”, which means the land of Lord Varaha. This shows that Varaha Swami lived in Tirumala even before Lord Venkateswara came. Later, Lord Venkateswara wanted to stay on Tirumala hills to help people during Kali Yuga (the present age). Before staying there, he asked permission from Lord Varaha, because Varaha Swami was already the owner of that land. Lord Varaha happily gave permission but made one small promise. He said that people should visit Varaha Swami first before going to Venkateswara Swami. Lord Venkateswara agreed to this promise. That is why many devotees believe that their Tirumala trip is not complete unless they first visit Varaha Swami Temple. Even today, many pilgrims follow this tradition with great faith. The temple is simple and peaceful. The prayers and rituals are done daily with devotion. Important festivals like Varaha Jayanti, Vaikuntha Ekadashi, and Brahmotsavam are celebrated here. During Brahmotsavam, a special holy bath called Chakra Snanam is done in Swami Pushkarini. People believe that praying to Varaha Swami helps remove sins and gives good health, happiness, and peace. Devotees also believe that Varaha Swami protects the Tirumala hills. In short, Sri Varaha Swami Temple is the starting point of Tirumala pilgrimage. Visiting this temple first shows respect, gratitude, and faith. That is why it holds a very special place in the hearts of devotees.
