About The Temple : Mukkoti Temple, officially known as Sri Agastheeswara Swamy Temple, is an ancient and sacred Lord Shiva temple located in Tirupati. It is one of the important Shiva temples in the town and holds great spiritual significance for local devotees.
History:
The temple is believed to be very old and is associated with the great sage Agastya (Agastya Maharshi).
According to legend:
*Sage Agastya performed severe penance at this place.
*Pleased with his devotion, Lord Shiva appeared in the form of a Shiva Lingam.
*The Lingam installed here is worshipped as Agastheeswara Swamy.
The temple reflects traditional South Indian (Dravidian) architectural style and has been a center of devotion for many generations.
Why It Is Called “Mukkoti”:
The word “Mukkoti” means three crores (30 million). It is believed that on sacred occasions, especially during Maha Shivaratri and other auspicious days, three crore devas (gods) worship Lord Shiva at this holy place. Because of this belief, the temple became popularly known as the Mukkoti Temple.
Temple Features:
*Main Deity: Sri Agastheeswara Swamy (Shiva Lingam)
*Separate shrine for Goddess Parvati (Ammavaru)
*Peaceful temple surroundings
*Daily poojas and special abhishekams
*Devotional atmosphere throughout the year
Festivals Celebrated:
*Maha Shivaratri – The most important festival, celebrated with night-long prayers and special rituals.
*Karthika Masam – Special deepams and poojas.
*Other Shiva-related festivals are also observed with devotion.
Timings:
6:00 AM: Temple opens & Abhishekam to Parivar Gods begins
6:00 AM – 7:00 AM: Abhishekam to family deities
7:00 AM: General Sarva Darshan starts
7:00 AM – 9:00 AM: Abhishekam to Swamy and Goddess
11:30 AM: Naivedyam (Food offering)
4:30 PM: Deepaaradhana & Naivedyam
7:30 PM: Ekantha Seva / Final Naivedyam before closing
*Note: The exact pooja times can vary slightly on festivals like Maha Shivaratri or Karthika masa days, so it’s good to check locally if you’re visiting then.
