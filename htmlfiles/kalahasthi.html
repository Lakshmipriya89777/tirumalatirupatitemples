About The Temple : Sri Kalahasteeswara Swamy Temple, popularly known as Srikalahasti Temple, is one of the most famous and ancient Shiva temples in India. It is located in Srikalahasti town, about 36 km from Tirupati in Tirupati district, Andhra Pradesh. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva, worshipped here as Kalahasteeswara, along with Goddess Gnanaprasunambika Devi. The temple is renowned as one of the Pancha Bhoota Stalams, representing the element of Air (Vayu). This makes it a highly significant pilgrimage center for Shaivites.
History of the Temple
Srikalahasti Temple has a rich history dating back over 1,000 years. The temple finds mention in ancient Puranas and Shaiva texts. It flourished under the patronage of the Chola, Pallava, Vijayanagara, and Chalukya dynasties. According to legend, the name Srikalahasti comes from Sri (Spider), Kala (Snake), and Hasti (Elephant). These three creatures were ardent devotees of Lord Shiva. Despite their natural enmity, they worshipped the Shiva Linga with great devotion. Pleased by their devotion, Lord Shiva granted them moksha, and the place came to be known as Srikalahasti. Another important legend states that Kannappa Nayanar, one of the 63 Shaiva saints, offered his eyes to the Shiva Linga out of pure devotion at this place. His devotion is considered one of the greatest examples of bhakti in Hindu tradition.
Religious Significance
Srikalahasti Temple is especially famous for Rahu–Ketu Sarpa Dosha Nivarana Pooja. Devotees from across India visit the temple to perform this ritual to overcome obstacles related to:
*Marriage delays
*Career and financial issues
*Health problems
*Ancestral doshas
The Vayu Linga here symbolizes the formless nature of Lord Shiva. Even today, a lamp placed near the sanctum flickers continuously, signifying the presence of air.
Architecture & Temple Features
The temple showcases classic Dravidian architecture. The main gopuram was constructed during the Vijayanagara period and stands about 120 feet tall. The temple complex includes beautifully carved pillars, mandapams, and inscriptions that reflect its long history. The temple is situated on the banks of the Swarnamukhi River, adding to its spiritual charm.
Festivals & Worship
Major festivals celebrated at Srikalahasti Temple include:
*Maha Shivaratri
*Brahmotsavam
*Kannappa Jayanthi
*Karthika Masam
During these festivals, the temple attracts thousands of devotees.
Importance in Tirupati Region
Srikalahasti Temple is considered a must-visit along with Tirumala Tirupati, as it represents the Shaiva tradition, complementing the Vaishnava worship of Lord Venkateswara.
Timings:
Temple Opens: 5:30 AM every day
General Darshan: 6:00 AM – 9:00 PM
Temple Closes: 9:00 PM
Special Ritual Timings
Rahu–Ketu Pooja: 6:00 AM – 6:00 PM daily
