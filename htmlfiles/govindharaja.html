About The Temple : Sri Govindaraja Swamy Temple is one of the most ancient and important temples in Tirupati, Andhra Pradesh. It is located in the heart of the city, near the Tirupati railway station and bus stand. The temple is dedicated to Lord Govindaraja Swamy, a form of Lord Vishnu, and is closely associated with Lord Venkateswara of Tirumala. The temple is administered by Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams (TTD) and plays a key role in the religious life of Tirupati.
History of the Temple
The Govindaraja Swamy Temple has a rich history dating back to the 12th century. It was originally consecrated by Saint Ramanujacharya, the great Vaishnavite philosopher and reformer. According to Vaishnava tradition, Ramanujacharya established the temple and introduced proper temple rituals and worship practices here. Legend says that Lord Govindaraja Swamy is the elder brother of Lord Venkateswara. It is believed that Lord Govindaraja Swamy came to Tirupati to manage the wealth and affairs of Lord Venkateswara, who resides on Tirumala hills. Because of this belief, many devotees visit Govindaraja Swamy Temple before or after Tirumala darshan.
Religious Significance
The temple holds great importance in Sri Vaishnavism. The main deity, Govindaraja Swamy, is seen in a reclining posture (Anantha Sayana) along with Goddess Lakshmi. The temple also houses shrines of Sri Andal, Sri Ramanujacharya, Sri Parthasarathi, and Sri Venkateswara. It is believed that worshipping Govindaraja Swamy brings:
*Peace and prosperity
*Family harmony
*Success in education and career
Architecture & Festivals
The temple is built in Dravidian architectural style with a magnificent seven-storied gopuram, which is one of the landmarks of Tirupati city. The temple complex includes beautiful mandapams, sculpted pillars, and sacred water tanks.Major festivals celebrated here include:
*Brahmotsavam
*Vaikunta Ekadasi
*Rathotsavam
*Sri Ramanuja Jayanthi
These festivals are celebrated with great devotion and attract thousands of devotees.
Role in Tirupati Pilgrimage
Govindaraja Swamy Temple is considered the starting point of the Tirumala pilgrimage. Traditionally, devotees offer prayers here before proceeding to Tirumala for Lord Venkateswara darshan.
