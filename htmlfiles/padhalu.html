About Srivari Padhalu : Srivari Padhalu is a sacred and historically significant shrine located on the northern side of the Tirumala hills, about 12 km from the main Tirumala temple. The name Srivari Padhalu literally means “the holy footprints of the Lord”, referring to the revered footprints of Lord Venkateswara (Sri Balaji) believed to mark His first step onto the Tirumala hills.
Mythological Significance
According to ancient Vaishnava tradition, Lord Venkateswara descended to earth and first set foot at this very spot before proceeding to Tirumala. The footprints enshrined here symbolize the Lord’s arrival, compassion, and accessibility to devotees. Because of this belief, Srivari Padhalu is considered one of the earliest sacred places connected to the Lord’s presence in Tirumala. Devotees regard the site as extremely auspicious and spiritually uplifting, representing humility and surrender at the feet of the Lord.
Historical Background
Srivari Padhalu has been revered for centuries and finds mention in traditional lore associated with Tirumala. The shrine gained prominence during the Vijayanagara period, when rulers and devotees actively supported Vaishnava worship and temple preservation in the region. Over time, a small temple structure was built to protect and honor the sacred footprints.
Temple & Surroundings
The shrine is simple and serene, reflecting deep devotion rather than architectural grandeur. It is set amid dense forests and rocky terrain, offering a peaceful and contemplative atmosphere. From the area near Srivari Padhalu, visitors can enjoy scenic views of the Tirumala hills, adding to the spiritual experience.
Spiritual Importance
Devotees visit Srivari Padhalu to seek:
*Blessings of Lord Venkateswara
*Spiritual peace and humility
*Relief from obstacles
*Strength in devotion
Many pilgrims include Srivari Padhalu as part of a Tirumala forest temple tour, considering it an important complement to their Tirumala darshan.
Present-Day Importance
Today, Srivari Padhalu is maintained by Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams (TTD) and is accessible through organized transport or forest route visits. Its calm environment, combined with deep spiritual symbolism, makes it a cherished destination for devotees seeking a quiet and meaningful spiritual experience.
