About The Temple : Sri Vakula Matha Temple is a sacred and emotionally significant temple located at Perur village, about 8–10 km from Tirupati, on the foothills of the Seshachalam range. The temple is dedicated to Sri Vakula Matha, revered as the foster mother of Lord Venkateswara (Sri Balaji). Because of this unique relationship, the temple holds a very special place in the spiritual tradition of Tirumala–Tirupati.
Mythological Background
According to legend, Vakula Matha was a devoted woman who raised Lord Venkateswara during His incarnation on earth. She cared for Him with motherly affection, fed Him, and guided Him. It is believed that even today, Lord Venkateswara offers the first naivedyam (food offering) to Vakula Matha before accepting it Himself at Tirumala, symbolizing gratitude and respect toward His foster mother. This deep bond makes Vakula Matha one of the most revered maternal figures in Vaishnavite tradition.
Historical Significance
The temple is believed to have ancient origins, with references linked to early Vaishnava traditions and later patronage during the Vijayanagara period. Over centuries, the site remained a quiet place of worship for devotees who wished to honor the divine mother of the Lord. The temple has been renovated and maintained in modern times to preserve its spiritual heritage while improving accessibility.
Temple Deity & Architecture
The presiding deity, Sri Vakula Matha, is worshipped in a calm and simple sanctum, reflecting her motherly and compassionate nature. The temple architecture is modest and traditional, blending naturally with the rural and hill surroundings, which creates a peaceful and devotional atmosphere.
Spiritual Importance
Devotees believe that praying at Vakula Matha Temple brings:
*Motherly blessings and protection
*Peace in family life
*Relief from worries and emotional distress
*Fulfillment of sincere wishes
Many pilgrims visit this temple before or after their Tirumala darshan, considering it auspicious to seek the blessings of Vakula Matha.
Present-Day Importance
Today, the Vakula Matha Temple is an important stop for devotees visiting Tirupati and Tirumala. Though less crowded than major temples, it attracts those seeking a quiet, devotional experience rooted in deep spiritual symbolism and maternal grace.
